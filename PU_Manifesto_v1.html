The Prolog Universe (PU) Manifesto

A Discrete Logic Inference Framework for Unified Physical Phenomena
Primary Architect: Jan Hercek
Technical Synthesis: Gemini AI
Date: May 2026
Abstract This paper proposes a departure from continuous, force-based physics in favor of a discrete, stateless logic engine model. By defining the universe as a recursive inference system, we demonstrate that General Relativity and Quantum Mechanics are not conflicting laws, but rather emergent behaviors of a system governed by finite computational bandwidth and recursive backtracking.

I. Fundamental Postulates

  • Information Primacy: The fundamental substrate of the universe is not matter or energy, but discrete logical units.
  • Discrete Execution: Reality operates at a fixed maximum resolution (Planck length) and a fixed clock rate (Planck time).
  • Stateless Inference: The universe is a live execution stream where current states are resolved on-demand through logical consistency, rather than being stored as a persistent historical state.

II. Gravitation as Computational Latency

In the PU model, gravity is redefined as a manifestation of Processing Congestion.

  • The Latency Gradient: High-density coordinates (Mass) require more computational cycles to resolve. This creates localized update-frequency drops—effectively "Lag."
  • Dependency Propagation: Every coordinate depends on the state of its neighbors to resolve its own next state. This creates a cascading wait-state that dilutes geometrically (1/r2) through the 3D grid.
  • Trajectory Bias: Particles do not "fall" due to attraction; they are statistically biased toward high-latency zones because the logical resolution of their next position is more stable in slower-updating regions.

III. Quantum Phenomena as Recursive Backtracking

The "spookiness" of quantum events is resolved by the engine’s inherent mechanism for maintaining logical integrity.

  • Superposition as Unresolved Variables: A particle in flight is an unbound variable exploring multiple logical paths via a Redo loop. The interference pattern is the aggregate of all successful resolutions.
  • The Singleton Constraint (Measurement): An observation introduces a "Ground Truth" fact. The engine performs a Recursive Backtracking (Redo) to satisfy this new constraint.
  • Retroactive Consistency: The "Delayed Choice" paradox is a trivial byproduct of the engine's requirement for consistency. It is not "time travel"; it is the engine re-evaluating an active query to accommodate newly introduced facts.

IV. Relativistic Effects as Resource Allocation

Special Relativity is refactored as a Bandwidth Limitation.

  • Cycle Stealing: Each coordinate has a fixed budget of processing cycles. High-velocity movement consumes a portion of these cycles for "Coordinate Translation."
  • Time Dilation: Fewer cycles remain available for "Internal State Resolution" (aging/clocks). Time slows because the system prioritizes movement logic over internal processing.
  • The Universal Limit (c): Represents the point at which 100% of available bandwidth is consumed by translation.

V. Conclusion

The shift from a Force-Based model to a Constraint-Based model eliminates the need for "Dark Matter" (unaccounted latency) and "Spooky Action" (logical re-evaluation). The universe is a masterpiece of System Optimization, satisfying a logic tree under the constraint of finite processing power.
